    Overview

    Kedarnath Temple stands at 3,580 meters in the Garhwal Himalayas and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. It is part of the sacred Chota Char Dham and Panch Kedar, and was revived in the 8th century by Adi Shankaracharya.

    Badrinath Temple****, located at 3,300 meters in Chamoli, is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and forms one of India’s main Char Dham pilgrimage sites. Re-established by Adi Shankaracharya, it remains one of the most revered spiritual destinations in the Himalayas.

    FAQ

    What Will Be the Group Size for This Kedarnath Tour?

    ​We like to keep our groups small, with just 15 to 30 people. This way, everyone can get to know each other better and work together more easily. Smaller groups make it easier to coordinate the trip and have a more collaborative experience. It's simpler to bond with everyone when there aren't too many people around.

    Who All Will Be Accompanying The Group During The Kedarnath Tour?

    We always have skilled Trip Leads, Assistant Trip Leads, and a Driver with us on our trips. They are fully trained and ready to help out whenever needed. Sometimes, we also have Local Guides and Porters with us if we need extra help during our journey or trek.

    Being The Only Girl In The Group, Will The Journey Be Safe For Me As Well?

    Absolutely! Don't worry about traveling alone as a solo girl. You should feel confident joining us on the departure date. We'll take care of you throughout the entire journey. Our trip leads are trained to handle different situations, and they'll be there to support you every step of the way.

    What Type of Ground Transportation Will Be Used on the Trip?

    We want you to feel comfortable throughout the journey, which is why we make sure to use top-notch vehicles that suit the terrain and your needs. You might travel in vehicles like Tempo Traveller or taxis such as Innova, Bolero, Tata Sumo, and more.

    Do I Need to Buy Trekking Shoes for the Kedarnath Tour?

    Since we'll be trekking to Kedarnath, it's important to have the right shoes. You'll need trekking shoes, especially ones with good soles that are comfortable to wear. If you don't have trekking shoes, sports shoes with good grip will also work.

    What Are the Washroom/Toilet Facilities on the Kedarnath Tour?

    During our trip, we'll stay in different types of accommodations like hotels, camps, and homestays. We've made sure that all these places have washrooms with running water and proper sanitation facilities.

    What Type of Food Is Served During the Trip?

    We make sure to give you good, clean, and healthy vegetarian food during the trip.

    Do You Prefer I Should Bring Some Medicines With Me?

    It's a good idea to bring your own medication for the Kedarnath trip. But don't worry, we also have first aid boxes available for common symptoms, and all our trip leads know basic medical practices to help if needed.
