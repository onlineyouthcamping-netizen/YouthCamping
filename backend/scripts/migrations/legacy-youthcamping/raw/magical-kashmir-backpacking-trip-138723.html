    Overview

    Kashmir, often referred to as "Paradise on Earth," is a region of breathtaking natural beauty nestled in the northernmost part of India. It is renowned for its picturesque landscapes, with majestic snow-capped mountains, lush green valleys, and serene lakes that captivate the senses. The iconic Dal Lake in Srinagar, adorned with floating gardens and intricate houseboats, is a quintessential symbol of the region. Kashmir is also steeped in rich cultural heritage, reflected in its diverse cuisine, handicrafts, and centuries-old traditions. However, the region has also witnessed periods of political and social challenges, adding a layer of complexity to its history. Despite this, Kashmir remains a destination of unparalleled allure, offering visitors a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural richness, and an opportunity for profound reflection on the complexities of this enchanting land.

    Day 01: Ahmedabad to Jammu Train Journey
    • Reporting venue will be shared , Assemble at the boarding point and start your night journey towards Jammu.
    • Experience a captivating 2-day train journey from Ahmedabad to Udhampur (Jammu) on Indian Railways, showcasing the rich diversity of cultures, landscapes, and people across Gujarat, Rajasthan, and Punjab, leading to the beautiful Union Territory of Jammu & Kashmir.
    • On the first day, stop at key stations like Abu Road and Jodhpur to savor local cuisine and immerse yourself in the unique flavors of these regions.

    Day 02: Reach Jammu & drive to Srinagar
    • Day 2 starts with a stunning sunrise and misty morning in Punjab, enjoying local Punjabi cuisine at renowned stations like Bhatinda, Ferozpur, and Jalandhar.
    • Arrive at Udhampur Railway Station by afternoon, concluding your memorable train journey.
    • At 3 PM, begin your road trip to Srinagar, traversing breathtaking landscapes that become increasingly beautiful as you near the city.
    • After a 6-hour drive, arrive in the enchanting city of Srinagar, where you'll check into your accommodation, freshen up, and relax.

    FAQ

    What Will Be the Group Size for This Tour?  

    ​We like to keep our groups small, with just 15 to 30 people. This way, everyone can get to know each other better and work together more easily. Smaller groups make it easier to coordinate the trip and have a more collaborative experience. It's simpler to bond with everyone when there aren't too many people around.

    Who All Will Be Accompanying The Group DuringTour?

    We always have skilled Trip Leads, Assistant Trip Leads, and a Driver with us on our trips. They are fully trained and ready to help out whenever needed. Sometimes, we also have Local Guides and Porters with us if we need extra help during our journey or trek, there will be no guide for 5-6 persons.

    Being The Only Girl In The Group, Will The Journey Be Safe For Me As Well?

    Absolutely! Don't worry about traveling alone as a solo girl. You should feel confident joining us on the departure date. We'll take care of you throughout the entire journey. Our trip leads are trained to handle different situations, and they'll be there to support you every step of the way.

    What Type of Ground Transportation Will Be Used on the Trip?

    We want you to feel comfortable throughout the journey, which is why we make sure to use top-notch vehicles that suit the terrain and your needs. You might travel in vehicles like Tempo Traveller or in taxis such as Innova, Bolero, Tata Sumo.

    What Are the Washroom/Toilet Facilities on the Himachal Tour?

    During our trip, we'll stay in different types of accommodations like hotels, camps, and homestays. We've made sure that all these places have washrooms with running water and proper sanitation facilities.

    What Type of Food Is Served During the Trip?

    We make sure to give you good, clean, and healthy vegetarian food during the trip.

    Do You Prefer I Should Bring Some Medicines With Me?

    It's a good idea to bring your own medication for the Himachal trip. But don't worry, we also have first aid boxes available for common symptoms, and all our trip leads know basic medical practices to help if needed.
